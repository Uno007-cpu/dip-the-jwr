The Silent Scream: Silindile's Story of Cyberbullying

A story of pain, resilience, and the importance of speaking up

Real People, Real Stories

Share to raise awareness:

Cyberbullying in South Africa: The Harsh Reality

1 in 3 young people have experienced cyberbullying

60% of victims never report the abuse to parents or authorities

Girls are twice as likely to be victims of cyberbullying

The Beginning

Silindile had always been the kind of student teachers noticed — not because she sought attention, but because of her quiet determination. She loved science, dreamed of becoming a doctor one day, and often helped her classmates with homework. Her confidence showed in the way she carried herself, until one day, it all began to change.

The Cyberbullying

It started small, with a sarcastic comment under one of her selfies: "Who told you you're pretty?" At first, Silindile laughed it off, but within days, the comments multiplied. Fake accounts appeared, sharing edited pictures of her, twisting her words, and mocking everything from her looks to her dreams. What hurt most was realizing that some of the bullies were people she thought were her friends.

Each notification became a knife to the chest.

Silindile stopped posting online, stopped answering group chats, and slowly even stopped going out with her real friends. She avoided mirrors, convinced she was the ugly person the internet painted her to be. At school, her grades slipped. She would sit in class, her mind not on equations or essays, but replaying the cruel words she read the night before. At home, her family noticed the silence at the dinner table, the forced smiles, and the way she stayed locked in her room.

Seeking Help

One afternoon, after a particularly cruel post spread through her grade, Silindile couldn't take it anymore. She broke down in tears in front of her favorite teacher, who immediately sat her down. For the first time, Silindile let the words pour out — the shame, the anger, the hopelessness. Her teacher didn't just listen, she acted. Together, they reported the accounts, blocked the bullies, and reached out to the school's counselor. Silindile also called a youth support hotline, where someone on the other end reminded her that she wasn't alone, and that healing was possible.

It took time, but Silindile began to rebuild. She leaned on her true friends, opened up to her family, and learned strategies to protect herself online. Most importantly, she realized that the cruel words said about her were never a reflection of her worth.

A Story of Resilience

Her story is not just one of pain, but of resilience. It is a reminder that cyberbullying is real, damaging, and often hidden. But it's also a reminder that speaking up can change everything. By standing together, showing kindness, and calling out cruelty, we can make the digital world safer for everyone.

Need Help? Resources Available

If you or someone you know is experiencing cyberbullying or GBV, help is available:

  • Cyberbullying Support: Report to platform administrators and block abusive accounts
  • GBV Command Centre: Call 0800 428 428 or dial *120*7867#
  • Lifeline: 0861 322 322
  • Childline: 116 (free from any phone)
  • South African Police Service: 10111

Remember, you are not alone, and there are people who want to help.
